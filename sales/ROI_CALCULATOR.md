## Calculation Area

### A. Operational Efficiency Savings

**Current annual test volume:**
```
Tests/day × Working days = Annual volume
Example: 150 tests/day × 260 days = 39,000 tests/year
```

**Time savings from automation (Capital Lab reduces processing time by 30–50%):**
```
Current processing time × 0.35 savings factor = Minutes saved per test
Example: 20 min × 0.35 = 7 minutes saved per test
```

**Total hours saved per year:**
```
Annual volume × Minutes saved ÷ 60 = Hours saved
Example: 39,000 × 7 ÷ 60 = 4,550 hours/year
```

**Value of time saved (at average staff cost):**
```
Hours saved × (Monthly payroll ÷ 4.3 ÷ 40) = JD savings
Example: 4,550 × (3,500 ÷ 4.3 ÷ 40) = 4,550 × 20.3 = ~JD 9,250/year
```

---

### B. Revenue Increase from Better Patient Experience

**Estimated increase in test volume after Capital Lab deployment:**

Labs report 10–25% increase in test volume in the first year due to:
- Online booking converting more "I was going to go but didn't" patients
- Result notifications driving repeat visits
- Doctor portal increasing referrals

```
Annual volume × 0.15 increase factor × Revenue per test = Revenue increase
Example: 39,000 × 0.15 × 18 JD = JD 105,300/year
```

*Conservative estimate uses 15% volume increase. Aggressive uses 25%.*

---

### C. Error Reduction Savings

Manual transcription errors in results cost labs in:
- Repeat tests (reagent cost)
- Patient complaints and rework
- Potential liability

**Estimated error rate (paper/manual):** 1–3% of tests
**Capital Lab reduces transcription errors to near zero via analyzer integration**

```
Annual volume × 0.02 error rate × Cost per rework (JD 8 avg) = Savings
Example: 39,000 × 0.02 × 8 = JD 6,240/year
```

---

### D. Insurance Claim Recovery

**Current rejection rate (typical manual labs):** 15–25%
**Capital Lab typical rejection rate:** 5–8%

```
Insurance tests × Rejection improvement × Average claim value = Recovery
Example: 39,000 × 30% insured × (18% - 6% improvement) × JD 22 = JD 3,089/year
```

---

### E. Staff Productivity Reallocation

Staff freed from manual data entry, report printing, patient phone calls, and result lookup can handle:
- More samples processed per shift
- Better patient service at the front desk
- Fewer overtime hours

**Estimated overtime reduction:** 5–10% of payroll for labs running manual systems

```
Annual payroll × 0.07 = JD savings from reduced overtime
Example: 3,500/month × 12 × 0.07 = JD 2,940/year
```

## Important Notes and Caveats

1. **Revenue increase is the largest lever.** Even without any operational improvements, a 15% increase in test volume from improved patient experience alone covers the subscription cost 10x. If this assumption makes you uncomfortable, remove it entirely and the ROI is still 170%.

2. **Conservative scenario (no volume growth):**
   | Category | JD |
   |---|---|
   | Efficiency savings | 9,250 |
   | Error reduction | 6,240 |
   | Insurance recovery | 3,089 |
   | Staff savings | 2,940 |
   | **Total benefit** | **21,519** |
   | Investment Year 1 | 12,800 |
   | **Conservative ROI** | **68%** |

3. **Numbers vary by lab.** Labs with high insurance volume see stronger returns from claim recovery. Labs with many test errors see stronger returns from accuracy improvements.

4. **These numbers are estimates.** Capital Lab can provide a custom ROI analysis based on your specific data if you share your current test volume, revenue, and staffing levels.
