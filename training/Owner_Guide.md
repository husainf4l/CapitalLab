# Capital Lab — Owner & Executive Guide
## Multi-Branch Analytics & Management · v1.0

---

## Your Role

As Owner or Executive, your view in Capital Lab is the highest level. You see consolidated performance across all branches, with real-time revenue, patient trends, and operational KPIs.

You do not manage daily operations — you set direction and measure results.

---

## 1. Login

1. Go to the Capital Lab URL → **"Login"**
2. Enter your owner email and password
3. Navigate to **Owner Dashboard** (`/owner`)

---

## 2. Navigation

**Owner Sidebar:**
- **Overview** — High-level KPIs across all branches
- **Revenue** — Detailed revenue analytics
- **Branches** — Branch performance comparison
- **Tests** — Test catalog management and revenue by test
- **Patients** — Patient growth and demographics
- **Inventory** — Cross-branch inventory summary
- **Insurance** — Cross-branch insurance portfolio
- **Executive** — Executive center with forecast and rankings

---

## 3. Overview Dashboard

The Overview is your morning dashboard.

**Key cards (top row):**
- **Total Revenue** — This month across all branches, with % change vs. last month
- **Samples Processed** — Volume indicator
- **Active Patients** — Unique patients seen this month
- **Open Invoices** — Outstanding amount to be collected

**Quick actions:**
- Click any branch name to drill into that branch's detail
- Click the revenue card to go to the full Revenue page

---

## 4. Revenue Analytics

### Revenue Page (`/owner/revenue`)
- **Total Revenue Chart** — Daily/weekly/monthly line chart
- **By Branch** — Revenue pie chart and table by branch
- **By Payment Method** — Cash vs. Card vs. Insurance split
- **By Test Category** — Which categories generate the most revenue

### Period Selection
Use the period tabs to select: **7 days / 30 days / 90 days**

### Reading the Chart
- The line shows daily revenue
- The trend line shows the direction
- A dip on weekends is normal in most labs
- Compare this month's curve to last month's by selecting the prior period

---

## 5. Branch Performance

### Branch Page (`/owner/branches`)
- Each branch shows: Revenue, Samples, Patients, Utilization %
- Branches are sortable by any metric
- Branch details expand to show daily breakdown

### Branch Ranking
The ranking shows which branches are performing strongest. Use this to:
- Identify underperforming branches for targeted intervention
- Recognize and replicate what high-performing branches are doing
- Make staffing and equipment allocation decisions

---

## 6. Executive Center

### Executive Page (`/owner/executive`)

The Executive Center is designed for board meetings and investor updates.

**KPI Cards (large numbers):**
- **Total Revenue** — Period total with growth percentage
- **Active Branches** — Branches with at least 1 transaction
- **Total Patients** — Cumulative patients served
- **Branch Rankings** — Quick count of ranked branches

**Branch Rankings Table:**
Shows top branches by revenue and sample volume. Use this for:
- Monthly performance reviews
- Branch manager accountability
- Resource planning

**Revenue Trend Chart:**
Bar chart showing daily revenue for the selected period (7 / 30 / 90 days). Use this for:
- Spotting seasonal patterns
- Identifying day-of-week trends
- Forecasting next month's revenue

---

## 7. Patient Analytics (`/owner/patients`)

- **New Patients** — First-time patients per period
- **Returning Patients** — Patients with 2+ visits
- **Demographics** — Age and gender breakdown
- **Top Referring Doctors** — Doctors who send the most patients (if tracked)

**Business use:** Returning patient rate is a key loyalty indicator. If it drops, investigate patient experience.

---

## 8. Test Performance (`/owner/tests`)

- **Top Tests by Volume** — Most ordered tests
- **Top Tests by Revenue** — Most profitable tests
- **Test Catalog Management** — Add or update tests (with admin permission)

**Business use:** If a low-volume test has high reagent cost, consider removing it from the catalog. If a high-revenue test is not being promoted, invest in awareness.

---

## 9. Insurance Portfolio (`/owner/insurance`)

- **Claims by Provider** — Volume and value per insurance company
- **Approval Rate** — % of claims approved
- **Average Claim Value** — Revenue per insurance patient
- **Outstanding Claims** — Total amount pending reimbursement

**Business use:** Low approval rates from a specific insurer may indicate a contract or coding issue. High outstanding claims may indicate a cash flow risk.

---

## 10. Key Business Metrics to Watch

| Metric | Good Signal | Warning Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue growth % | > 5%/month | Flat or negative |
| Insurance approval rate | > 85% | < 70% |
| Returning patient rate | > 40% | < 20% |
| Outstanding invoices | < 15% of revenue | > 30% of revenue |
| Sample volume vs. last month | Growing or stable | Declining 2+ months |

---

## 11. Best Practices

- **Review Executive Center every Monday morning** — 5 minutes gives you the week's direction
- **Monthly branch manager meetings** — use the Branch Performance data as the agenda
- **Quarterly full review** — revenue, patients, insurance, inventory — compare to same quarter last year
- **Insurance claims** — review outstanding balance monthly; aging claims lose value

---

## 12. Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Revenue numbers seem too low | Check date range; confirm it is not filtered to one branch |
| A branch is missing from the list | Check if branch is marked Active in branch settings |
| Cannot see all branches | Confirm your account has Owner role; contact Capital Lab support |
| Report does not match my accountant's figures | Check for outstanding/unpaid invoices vs. collected revenue |
