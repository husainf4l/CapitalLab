# Capital Lab — Reception & Front Desk Guide
## Daily Workflow Reference · v1.0

---

## Your Role

As a Receptionist, you are the first point of contact for patients. Your responsibilities in Capital Lab include:

- Registering new patients
- Confirming appointments and checking patients in
- Creating and managing test orders
- Processing payments
- Printing barcode labels and invoices
- Answering basic patient portal questions

---

## 1. Login

1. Go to the Capital Lab URL
2. Click **"Login"**
3. Enter your staff email and password
4. You will be directed to the **Lab Dashboard**

---

## 2. Starting Your Day

**Daily morning checklist:**
- [ ] Check today's appointment list: **Appointments** → filter by today's date
- [ ] Note any VIP or priority patients flagged by the manager
- [ ] Confirm printer is connected and has labels/paper
- [ ] Check unread notifications for any urgent messages

---

## 3. Patient Registration — Walk-In

When a patient arrives without an appointment:

1. Go to **Lab Dashboard** → **Orders** → **"New Order"**
2. Search for existing patient: enter name, national ID, or phone
   - **If found:** Select the patient and proceed
   - **If new:** Click **"New Patient"** and enter details:
     - Full name (Arabic and English)
     - Date of birth
     - National ID or Passport
     - Mobile phone
     - Gender
     - Address (optional)
     - Insurance details (if applicable)
3. Select tests ordered (search by name or code)
4. Select branch (if multi-branch)
5. Review order summary
6. Click **"Create Order"**
7. **Print barcode label** — click the print button immediately after order creation

---

## 4. Appointment Check-In

When a patient arrives for a scheduled appointment:

1. Go to **Lab Dashboard** → **Appointments**
2. Find the patient's appointment (search by name or appointment number)
3. Click **"Check In"** → status changes to "Arrived"
4. The order is automatically created from the appointment
5. Print the barcode label
6. Direct the patient to the waiting area / phlebotomy room

---

## 5. Creating an Order

Orders can be created for:
- Walk-ins (manually)
- Booked appointments (automatically)
- Add-on tests for a current patient

### To add tests to an existing order:
1. Find the existing order
2. Click **"Add Test"**
3. Search and add the test
4. Save changes
5. Print new or updated barcode label

---

## 6. Printing Barcode Labels

Every sample needs a unique barcode label before collection.

1. After creating an order, click **"Print Label"**
2. Ensure the label printer is selected (check with your manager for the correct printer name)
3. Confirm label printed correctly (patient name, test names, barcode visible)
4. Attach label to the sample tube before the patient goes for collection

---

## 7. Processing Payments

### Cash Payment
1. Open the order or invoice
2. Click **"Record Payment"**
3. Select **"Cash"**
4. Enter the amount received
5. System calculates change
6. Click **"Confirm Payment"**
7. Print receipt (optional)

### Card Payment
1. Process the card through the POS terminal
2. In Capital Lab, click **"Record Payment"**
3. Select **"Card"**
4. Enter the amount
5. Click **"Confirm Payment"**

### Insurance Payment
1. When patient presents insurance card:
   - Enter insurance provider and policy number in the patient profile (if not already there)
2. Create the order normally
3. When invoicing, select **"Insurance"** as payment method
4. The invoice is sent to the insurance queue (Admin → Insurance → Claims)
5. Collect any patient co-payment separately (if applicable)

---

## 8. Handling Insurance Patients

1. Ask for insurance card at reception
2. Check if the lab has a contract with that insurance provider (ask your manager)
3. Enter insurance details in the patient's profile
4. Create the order as normal
5. In the payment step, select the insurance provider and enter the authorization number if provided
6. Note any patient co-payment percentage
7. Collect the co-payment in cash/card at reception

---

## 9. Issuing Invoices

1. Go to the patient's order
2. Click **"Generate Invoice"** (if not auto-generated)
3. Review all line items and amounts
4. Click **"Issue Invoice"**
5. Print or send via email/WhatsApp to the patient

---

## 10. Patient Portal Help

Patients may ask you about the portal. Common requests:

| Patient Request | What to Do |
|---|---|
| "How do I see my results?" | Direct them to the portal; help them log in or create an account |
| "I didn't get my results notification" | Check if their mobile number is correct in their profile |
| "Can I download my report?" | Show them the Results section in the portal; confirm doctor has approved |
| "I forgot my password" | Direct them to "Forgot Password" on the login page |

---

## 11. Best Practices

- **Never enter a test without a clear request** from the patient or doctor — verify verbally if unclear
- **Always print the barcode before the patient goes for collection** — missing labels cause delays
- **Double-check insurance details** before creating the order — errors cause claim rejections later
- **Be friendly about the patient portal** — it reduces your phone call volume significantly

---

## 12. Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Patient not found in search | Try national ID; try partial name; if new, create new patient |
| Barcode label not printing | Check printer connection; check printer selection in browser |
| Payment not processing | Check internet connection; verify amount and method |
| Test not found in search | Try partial code or name; if genuinely missing, contact manager |
| Cannot check in appointment | Check if appointment is for today and this branch |

---

**Escalate to Lab Manager if:** patient disputes pricing, insurance authorization unclear, system is down, or you are unsure about any clinical test detail.
